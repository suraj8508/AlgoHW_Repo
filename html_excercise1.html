My Birth Place

Alappuzha "you better visit one time in your life"

Alleppey, which is also called as Alappuzha, is a coastal district of Kerala. Alleppey become famous for many reasons like the beautiful combination of lakes, lagoons, crisscrossed narrow canals, rivers, coconut palms, paddy fields and many more. Earlier Alleppey and near by areas are low laying wet lands. These wet lands are part of Vembanadu lake which originates from Astamudi Lake at Quilon till Kochi. The Maharaja of erstwhile Travancore state has given permission to reclaim these wetlands and converted these wetlands to fertile paddy fields due to increasing needs of rice for local people. Reclamation of wetland system started in 1832 and by the end of 1984 approx 2300 hectors of land has been reclaimed. Two thirds of the total vembanadu lake has been reclaimed or converted in to paddy fields. When this vast lake reclaimed and converted to paddy fields, many big and small canals and rivers are formed. More than half of this area is now known as Kuttanad, which has vast paddy fields, lagoons, small canals and rivers. Most of the small and narrow canals are located in Kuttanad. Alleppey, being a coastal district one side of Alleppey is beautiful canals and rivers and other side is the Arabian Sea which is Alleppey beach.

Major Attraction of Alappuzha

Backwater Beach House Boats
The Alappuzha backwaters was earlier used as a mode of transportation, fishing and agriculture and have evolved over time as a tourism hotspot. The main attraction of the Alappuzha backwaters is the overnight journey in the famous Kerala houseboats. This beautiful beach with a silver stretch of sand surrounded by palm grooves is one of the most popular tourist attraction of the district and Kerala. Alapuzzha was coined as the Venice of the East by the travelers who come here across the world. Beach has many attractions nearby . Houseboats in Kerala or Kettuvallams were traditionally cargo boats that carried rice, spices and other commodities, which now are transformed into luxury boats complete with bedrooms, kitchen, living area, bathrooms and other amenities. Today, these allow travellers to enjoy the breath-taking beauty of Alappuzha while gliding along its backwaters.

How to Reach Alappuzha from Cochin(cok) Airport

BY Car

  • Head south-east on Aroor – Thoppumpady Road towards Post Office Road.
  • Turn right onto NH47. Turn left at Convent Square Junction onto Cullen Road.
  • Alappuzha is about 53 kilometres away from Kochi and it takes 1 hour and 40 minutes to reach Alappuzha from the city.

BY Train

  1. Indian Railways operates a train from Ernakulam Jn to Alappuzha hourly.
  2. Operated by Indian Railways, the Kochi to Alappuzha train service departs from Ernakulam Jn and arrives in Alappuzha.
  3. The train journey time between Kochi and Alappuzha is around 47 min and covers a distance of around 36 miles.
  4. Typically 98 trains run weekly, although weekend and holiday schedules can vary so check in advance.
  5. Tickets cost $1 - $8 and the journey takes 47 min.

BY Bus

  1. Kerala State Road Transport Corporation operates a bus from Thrissur KSRTC to Alapuzzha 5 times a day.
  2. Tickets cost $1 - $3 and the journey takes 3h 20m.
